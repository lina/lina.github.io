Books

When not working, I almost always have airpods in my ears playing an audiobook or a podcast!

Favorite non-fictional books:

The Snowball: Warren Buffett and the Business of Life by Alice Shroeder

Reading through pages and pages of earnings reports may not be everyone’s thing, but it’s Warren Buffet’s thing. I love the fact he tap-dances to work everyday.

High Output Management by Andy Grove

Like Andy, I have a chemical engineering background. His systematic and methodical way of thinking is a great way to approach any problem!

Losing My Virginity: How I've Survived, Had Fun, and Made a Fortune Doing Business My Way by Richard Branson

Richard Branson’s adrenaline seeking spirit combined with his business drive is amazing. Having fun and building a business goes hand in hand!

"Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!": Adventures of a Curious Character by Richard Feynman

One of my first pandemic reads. Inspired me to always be curious.

Delivering Happiness: A Path to Profits, Passion, and Purpose by Tony Hsieh

I love Tony's take on company culture and customer service. Customer obsession is key!

Trillion Dollar Coach: The Leadership Playbook of Silicon Valley's Bill Campbell by Eric Schmidt, Jonathan Rosenberg, Alan Eagle

The fact that one person can have such a big influence on so many leaders of big players in Silicon Valley is remarkable!

Steve Jobs by Walter Isaacson

I read this in 2014 while on a 100 day backpacking trip through Latin America! The very next year I found my way to Silicon Valley!

Favorite fictional books:

Sphere by Michael Crichton

This was the book that sucked me into the Michael Crichton world beyond Jurassic Park.

小灵通漫游未来 by 叶永烈

This is a Chinese sci-fi book with robots, flying cars, time travel, buildings with drawer-like apartments, life extension, human aging to several centuries old, brain computer interfaces, brain augmentation, etc. I read this as a kid around 2000. I was immersed in a futuristic world that is actually happening today!
